The Second Code takes the advantages of high-performance computations on the existing GPU clusters to analyze and interpret the genetic data. Second Code creates a healthcare software platform which can effectively analyze large amounts of genetic data to solve bioinformatics and clinical sequencing problems. Having these technologies would allow fast and cost- efficient analysis of genetic data.

Genomic data grows exponentially driven by dramatically declining costs and FDA approval of genetic tests: from 1000 human genomes sequenced in 2010 to 15,000 in 2012, and the prognosis for 2016 is 25M genomes. According to Transparency Market Research (2013) global market for bioinformatics will reach $8,3B in 2016 with CAGR=25%. Despite this fact, genomic data analysis is still expensive, slow and complex: CLC Bio desktop software costs $10K per user per year and takes 24h to analyze 1 human genome. With DNANexus and EasyGenomics it costs $1400+ per genome and takes several hours.

FAQ

What are the advantages of GPU cluster infrastructure?
Graphics Processing Units (GPU) have a parallel structure that makes them more effective than general-purpose CPUs for algorithms where processing of large blocks of data is done in parallel.
How fast can genome assebly be?
In public beta-version (expected soon) one human genome analysis (from 40x coverage raw data to medical report) will take about a half an hour and cost only $150.
